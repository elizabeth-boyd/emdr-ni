Eye Movement desensitisation and Reprocessing-----What is it?

Eye movement desensitization and reprocessing (EMDR)

is a psychotherapy technique that can help people heal from distressing life experiences and trauma. It involves recalling a traumatic event while moving your eyes in a specific way, usually following the movement of your therapist's finger.
EMDR is a structured therapy that typically consists of 6–12 sessions, one or two times per week. The therapy has eight phases, including:
Initial history discovery and treatment planning Preparation
Assessment
Desensitization
Installation
Body scan
Closure
Reevaluation
EMDR can help people:
Process negative images, emotions, beliefs, and body sensations associated with traumatic memories
See things from a different perspective
Relieve symptoms
EMDR is considered generally safe and has been proven effective in many studies. It can produce positive results faster than some other types of mental health therapy.
EMDR can be used to treat a variety of issues, including:
Eating disorders
Grief and loss
Pain
Performance anxiety
Personality disorders
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
